# Amazon Behavioral / Leadership Principles Interview Question Bank

This document groups **commonly asked Amazon behavioral interview questions** by **Leadership Principle (LP)**. Use it as a **practice repository**: map each question to 1–2 of your strongest STAR stories.

---

## Customer Obsession

Tell me about a time when:

* You went above and beyond for a customer.
* You had to balance competing customer needs.
* You had to say no to a customer request and explain why.
* You anticipated a customer’s need before they explicitly asked.

---

## Ownership

Tell me about a time when:

* You took responsibility beyond your defined job scope.
* You owned a project end-to-end.
* You took responsibility for something that went wrong.

---

## Invent & Simplify

Tell me about a time when:

* You came up with a simple solution to a complex problem.
* You invented something new or significantly improved an existing process.
* You simplified a system or process others thought couldn’t be simplified.

---

## Are Right, A Lot

Tell me about a time when:

* You made a decision with incomplete or limited data.
* You were wrong about something — what happened and what you learned.
* You questioned an assumption that everyone else accepted.

---

## Learn and Be Curious

Tell me about a time when:

* You had to learn a new technology quickly.
* You taught yourself something to improve your work.
* Your curiosity led to a better technical or business decision.

---

## Hire and Develop the Best

Tell me about a time when:

* You coached or mentored someone effectively.
* You helped someone raise their performance.
* You gave difficult feedback to help someone grow.

---

## Insist on the Highest Standards

Tell me about a time when:

* You refused to accept poor quality even under pressure.
* You raised the bar for your team’s output.
* You improved a standard that others thought was already sufficient.

---

## Think Big

Tell me about a time when:

* Your vision led to measurable impact.
* You proposed something bold or ambitious.
* You went beyond the initial scope of a project.

---

## Bias for Action

Tell me about a time when:

* You made a fast decision despite risk or ambiguity.
* You took action without full approval.
* You solved a problem before anyone explicitly asked you to.

---

## Frugality

Tell me about a time when:

* You achieved results with limited resources.
* You delivered efficiently without additional budget.
* You solved a problem without asking for more headcount or tools.

---

## Earn Trust

Tell me about a time when:

* You had to rebuild trust with a peer, manager, or stakeholder.
* You delivered difficult feedback respectfully.
* You handled pushback while maintaining strong working relationships.

---

## Dive Deep

Tell me about a time when:

* You identified a hidden root cause of a problem.
* You dug into data or logs to prove or disprove an assumption.
* You encountered conflicting indicators and resolved them.

---

## Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit

Tell me about a time when:

* You disagreed with a peer or superior and why.
* You convinced others to change direction.
* You stood by your analysis despite resistance, and then committed.

---

## Deliver Results

Tell me about a time when:

* You delivered under tight deadlines and adversity.
* You had to pivot because your original plan was failing.
* You juggled multiple difficult assignments simultaneously.

---

## Additional Real‑World Behavioral Questions

Tell me about a time when:

* You had to negotiate a win‑win outcome.
* Your project failed and what you learned from it.
* You managed conflicting priorities.
* You balanced speed with accuracy.
* You influenced decisions without formal authority.

---

### How to Use This Document

* Do **not** prepare one story per question.
* Prepare **2–3 strong stories per LP** and reuse them with different emphasis.
* Focus on **problem → insight → decision → trade‑off → result**.
* Always quantify impact where possible.

This document is meant to be a **living reference** — add notes under each question mapping it to your personal STAR stories.
